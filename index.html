Site Enhancements

Creighton University

Improved Program Navigation
The table on each subdomain would make a user travel back to www.creighton.edu, or stay within www.creighton.edu which is highly undergrad focused. Now when a user selects a grad program they are sent to the grad school subdomain. The same goes for business programs.

Conditional Request Form Buttons
A program page has a button called request for information, but there are three request information forms created by the university. An overall one, a Heider College of Business one and a Grad one. I created javascript code to detect the subdomain and send them to the correct RFI. Instead of a user being on gradschool.creighton.edu and clicking on a business program then being directed to Heider request information form. When they may not of been introduced who or what Heider is.

Linking to an Open Accordion
University Communication team often uses accordions on the website. Our team wanted to link to an open one in an email communication, but the feature wasn't available. I created code, that could be added to the page for this to happen, and how-to's so other teams could use it as well.

Me Myself and Design

Event Chair - AIGA Nebraska

After being away for almost two years AIGA Nebraska sought out to bring back Me, Myself, and Design MMD a student focused design conference, to it's yearly programming. I was voted on to organize and execute the event for 2016. The limitations I was given were: that it had to be in Lincoln, Nebraska, 45 minutes away from where I reside, or halfway in between Omaha and Lincoln.Additionally the budget was set at $1200.00 already. With the experience of not only attending MMD in prior years but also previously residing on the board, I set a handful of my own goals to help direct my decision making. They were as follows:

  • Diverse speakers – haven't spoken for AIGA Nebraska before, broad range of subjects, range in experience, gender, and other demographics.
  • Diverse attendees – University and community College students, different years in schooling, and other other demographics.
  • Inclusive food choices – providing a variety of choices for attendees throughout the day
  • No additional costs – the ticket to cover attendees needs for the day, including parking.
  • Reusable Logo – years prior recreated a logo and accompanying materials each year, taking a lot of time and resources. I wanted to have a logo that could/would be used year after year and alleviate that pain point.

Each goal was met, with the additional accomplishments of securing the location and food under budget. Obtaining 10 portfolio reviewers from local agencies. Having three keynote speakers and 10 additional panel speakers throughout the day. As well as providing art direction for all materials and mentoring the co-chair for when they lead the following year.
